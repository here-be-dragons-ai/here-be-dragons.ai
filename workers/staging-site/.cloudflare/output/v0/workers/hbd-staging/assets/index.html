At the edge of the map

Where the map grows thin.

Here be dragons — Hic sunt dracones — comes from early cartography: a mark drawn near the edge of the known world.

It became a metaphor for terrain where the map grows thin, the risks are real, and curiosity only works with humility. That is the spirit of this place — clear minds at the frontier, walking carefully.
